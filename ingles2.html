What is an eating disorder?

An eating disorder is an obsession with food and weight that harms a well-being of the person. Although we all worry about our weight sometimes, People who have an eating disorder go to extremes to avoid gaining weight. There are w main eating disorders: anorexia nervosa and bulimia What is anorexia? Anorexia nervosa is a disease that usually occurs in adolescents, but it can also occur in adolescent children and in adult women and men. People that

have anorexia are obsessed with being thin. They don't want to eat and they are afraid of gaining weight. They may constantly worry about how how many calories you eat or how much fat is in your food. can take diet pills, laxatives or diuretics to lose weight. they can exercise too a lot. People with anorexia often think they are fat even though they are very thin They can lose so much weight that it looks like they are sick.
Anorexia is not just a problem with food or weight. It is an attempt to use diet and weight to deal with emotional problems.
What is bulimia?

Bulimia is eating a lot of food at once (called bingeing) and then throwing or using laxatives to remove food from the body (called purging). After a binge, some bulimics fast (not eat) or exercise too much to maintain of gaining weight. People who have bulimia can also use diuretics, laxatives or diet pills to "control" your weight. People who have bulimia they often try to hide their binges and purges.
